Specialist cardiologists

Our doctors

Dr Nick Fisher

MBBS MRCP FRACP FCSANZ MD
Founder & Clinical Director · Interventional cardiologist

Dr Fisher is the founder and clinical director of Top of the South Cardiology, and serves as Head of the Department of Cardiology at Nelson Marlborough DHB. His clinical expertise spans cardiac imaging via CT and coronary angiography, stent and pacemaker insertion, cardiovascular risk assessment, valvular heart disease, and arrhythmias.

Prior to relocating to New Zealand in 2006, Dr Fisher served as Cardiologist for the Royal Navy (rank: Surgeon Commander) and trained at the Royal Brompton Hospital in London — one of the world's leading cardiac centres. He saw operational service in the Falklands, Gulf, and Adriatic.

Dr Fisher is the author of Frontline Cardiology and co-authored New Zealand's Expected Standards of Access to Secondary Care. Outside the clinic, he enjoys fishing and vineyard management.

Dr Tammy Pegg

MBChB (Honours) MRCP FRACP FCSANZ DPhil
Heart failure & non-invasive imaging specialist

Dr Pegg graduated with honours from the University of Leeds in 2000 and completed her basic medical training at University of Manchester Hospitals. She became a Member of the Royal College of Physicians in 2003 and began advanced cardiology training in the Wessex Deanery in 2004.

She holds a DPhil (PhD) from the University of Oxford (2008), where her research focused on improving cardiac surgical techniques and using cardiac MRI for heart function assessment. Her doctoral work earned her recognition as a finalist in both the Vivenne Thomas American Heart Association Young Investigator Award and the New Zealand Heart Foundation Young Investigator Award.

Her clinical specialties include heart failure, non-invasive imaging (CT and cardiac MRI), cardiovascular risk assessment, valvular disease, and palpitations. Dr Pegg has been a passionate advocate for public access defibrillators across the region.

Dr Niall Foley

MB ChB BAO FRACP
Electrophysiology & cardiac devices

Originally from Ireland, Dr Foley completed his undergraduate degree from University College Dublin with honours, followed by early postgraduate training at the Mater Hospital in Dublin. He relocated to New Zealand in 2011 and completed his advanced cardiology training in Wellington before completing a specialist fellowship in electrophysiology and cardiac devices.

Dr Foley's clinical focus is on electrophysiology and cardiac pacing — the diagnosis and management of complex heart rhythm disorders. He places particular emphasis on shared decision-making and patient education, ensuring his patients understand their condition and the options available to them.

Outside medicine, he enjoys football, running, snow sports, and travel.

Clinical & support team

The people behind your care

Our cardiologists are supported by a highly experienced clinical team with decades of combined expertise in cardiac technology, nursing, and patient care.

Kate Fisher

Practice Manager

Often the first point of contact, Kate brings a nursing background and deep familiarity with cardiac care to her role managing the practice. She guides patients through every stage — consultation, investigation, and follow-up.

Steve White

Senior Sonographer

With over 35 years of experience across New Zealand, the UK, and Australia, Steve is arguably one of the most experienced cardiac sonographers in the country. He is dual-trained in paediatric and adult echocardiography and brings a radiography and general ultrasound background to his work — and a genuine passion for his profession.

Sarah Dixon

Cardiology Specialist Nurse

Sarah brings over 35 years as a registered nurse, including 22 years as a cardiology specialist. She completed her master's degree in 2015 and holds additional postgraduate qualifications. Her focus is patient assessment, management, and education — helping people truly understand their heart health and the reasons behind their treatment.

Kirstie Williams

Charge Nurse Manager — Interventional Suite

Originally from Wales, Kirstie completed her nursing degree in Nelson before pursuing a cardiology career at Wellington's catheterisation laboratory. She returned to Nelson's cath lab to take on the charge nurse role, and is passionate about delivering gold standard patient care in our interventional suite.

Jobeth Lancaster

Senior Technologist — Nelson

A clinical physiologist with a postgraduate diploma in medical technology, Jobeth is trained in cardiac and respiratory testing. She conducts treadmill and lung function testing with a commitment to providing quality clinical results to support physician decision-making.

Sarah Webby

Senior Technologist — Wairau

Sarah trained in physiology in Christchurch with a focus on electrophysiology, and worked at one of England's largest NHS trusts before joining our team. She manages pacemaker care and Holter monitoring at Wairau Hospital, providing specialist cardiac support to Marlborough-based patients.

Debbie Hampson

Senior Technologist

Debbie greets patients for treadmill testing and brings extensive experience ensuring every test provides clinically useful, high-quality information for our cardiologists.

Affiliations & accreditation

Who we work with

Nelson Marlborough Health

We work closely with Nelson Marlborough Health, providing specialist cardiac services to both privately referred and publicly funded patients across the region. Dr Fisher serves as Head of Department of Cardiology.

Southern Cross Affiliated Provider

We are an affiliated provider with Southern Cross Health Society, meaning most members can access our services without pre-authorisation for standard consultations and investigations.

RACP & CSANZ Members

Our cardiologists hold Fellowship of the Royal Australasian College of Physicians (FRACP) and are members of the Cardiac Society of Australia and New Zealand (CSANZ).
