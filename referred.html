For patients

You've been referred —
here's what happens next

You don't need to do anything right now. We'll contact you directly once we've reviewed your referral.

Please don't phone us to follow up

We receive every referral and review them in order of clinical priority. We contact every patient once we've assessed the referral — usually within a few days. If your situation changes urgently, please contact your GP.

Your journey

What to expect, step by step

Most people find that knowing what's coming makes the whole experience less daunting. Here's exactly what happens from referral to results.

  1. 1

    Your GP sends a referral

    Your GP writes a referral letter describing your symptoms and medical history, and sends it to our clinic. You don't need to do anything at this point — your GP handles this step.

  2. 2

    We review it within a few days

    One of our cardiologists reviews your referral, assesses its urgency, and determines what investigation or consultation is most appropriate for you.

  3. 3

    We contact you directly

    We'll phone or write to you to arrange your appointment or tests. We'll tell you where to go, how long it will take, and how to prepare. If you have questions at this point, we're happy to help.

  4. 4

    Your appointment or tests

    You'll attend your appointment at one of our Nelson or Marlborough clinics. Depending on your referral, this may be a consultation with a cardiologist, one or more tests, or both. You're welcome to bring a support person.

  5. 5

    Your results

    After your appointment or tests, our team will discuss your results with you — either at the same visit or at a follow-up appointment. A written report also goes to your GP so your whole care team stays informed.

Practical information

Before your appointment

Can I bring someone?

Yes, and we encourage it. Having a support person with you can help you take in what's discussed. Please let us know when you book so we can make sure there's space.

What should I bring?

Bring a list of your current medications, any previous test results or ECGs you have, and your NHI number. If you wear glasses, bring them — we may ask you to read some forms.

How long will it take?

A consultation is usually 30–60 minutes. Some tests can take longer — we'll tell you how to plan your day when we contact you to arrange your appointment.

Should I stop my medications?

Don't stop any medication before your appointment unless your GP or our team specifically asks you to. We'll give you any specific instructions when we book you in.

What will it cost?

Costs depend on whether you're covered by Southern Cross or another insurer, self-funding, or in some cases publicly funded. We'll clarify costs when we contact you to book.

What should I wear?

Comfortable clothing is ideal. For some tests — like an echocardiogram or stress ECG — you may need to remove your top, so a loose shirt or blouse is easiest.

Funding your care

Insurance, self-funding & public referrals

Most of our patients are either covered by health insurance (most commonly Southern Cross) or self-funding. A smaller number of investigations are publicly funded.

We are a Southern Cross Affiliated Provider — which means Southern Cross members don't require pre-authorisation for most consultations and standard tests.

With health insurance

A GP or practice nurse referral is still required. Confirm with your insurer that your procedure or consultation has been authorised if in doubt. Southern Cross members generally don't need pre-authorisation for our standard services.

Self-funding (no insurance)

Many patients choose to self-fund reassurance investigations. We'll be transparent about costs upfront. Consultations, standard tests like a 24-hour ECG, echocardiogram, and exercise stress tests can all be self-funded.

Public referrals

Some publicly funded investigations are arranged through Nelson Marlborough Health. Your GP will advise if this applies to your situation. Please note: we cannot accept self-referrals — all patients require a GP or specialist referral.
