For GPs & referrers

Referring a patient to
Top of the South Cardiology

We make it straightforward to refer your patients to us. Here's what we need and how the process works.

Referral process

How to refer

We accept referrals by letter, fax, or electronic referral. Once we receive your referral, we contact the patient directly to arrange their appointment.

  1. 1

    Write your referral letter

    Include the clinical information listed below. The more complete your letter, the faster we can triage and arrange appropriate care for your patient.

  2. 2

    Send it to us

    Send by letter, fax, or electronic referral to our Nelson clinic. Our contact details are below. We do not require a referral form — a letter is sufficient.

  3. 3

    We triage and contact the patient

    A cardiologist reviews every referral. We contact the patient directly to arrange their appointment — you don't need to do anything further. We'll send you a written report after every consultation.

Referral content

What to include in your letter

A well-completed referral allows us to triage more accurately and prepare appropriately for your patient's appointment.

Essential information

  • Patient name, date of birth, NHI, and contact details
  • Presenting symptoms and their duration
  • Relevant past medical history and cardiac risk factors
  • Current medications
  • Your specific clinical question or reason for referral
  • Funding pathway: Southern Cross, other insurer, self-funded, or public

Helpful extras

  • Recent ECG, blood pressure readings, or echo results if available
  • Relevant blood results (lipids, HbA1c, renal function)
  • Any urgency or timing considerations
  • Patient's preferred location (Nelson or Marlborough)

Important: we cannot accept self-referrals

All patients must be referred by a GP or specialist. If a patient contacts us directly, we'll ask them to see their GP first.

Urgent referrals

If your patient requires urgent cardiac assessment, please call our clinic directly rather than sending a letter. For life-threatening emergencies, contact 111 or send the patient to the nearest emergency department.

What happens after you refer

We review every referral within a few working days. We contact the patient directly to arrange their appointment. You'll receive a written report after every consultation or investigation — this is sent to your practice address.

Southern Cross affiliation

We are a Southern Cross Affiliated Provider for most standard consultations and investigations. Southern Cross members generally don't need pre-authorisation, but advise your patients to confirm with their insurer if in doubt.

Our capabilities

What we can offer your patients

We provide a comprehensive range of outpatient cardiac investigations and consultations, all locally in Nelson and Marlborough.

Imaging

Echocardiography, CT coronary angiography, and calcium scoring.

Rhythm monitoring

24-hour Holter ECG, extended patch monitoring, and event recorders.

Functional testing

Exercise stress testing, pharmacological stress echocardiography.

Invasive procedures

Coronary angiography, PCI, stenting, and pacemaker implantation — in our purpose-built cath lab.

Occupational medicals

Aviation medicals, fitness-to-drive assessments, and cardiac clearances for commercial and professional purposes.

Remote consultations

Video and phone consultations available for follow-up and results, particularly for Marlborough patients.

Specialist cardiology for the Nelson and Marlborough regions. Serving our community for over 20 years.
