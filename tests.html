Outpatient tests

Cardiac investigations —
what each test involves

Plain-language explanations of every test we offer: what it is, whether it's uncomfortable, how long it takes, and how to prepare.

Most tests are straightforward and non-invasive

Many of the investigations we offer involve no needles, no pain, and can be done in under an hour. We'll give you specific preparation instructions when we contact you to book.

Imaging

Heart imaging tests

Ultrasound

Echocardiogram

An ultrasound scan of your heart. Sound waves create a moving image of your heart's structure and how it pumps — showing the size of chambers, valve function, and how well the heart muscle works. It's the most commonly performed cardiac investigation.

Duration30–45 minutes
Comfortable?Yes — painless
PreparationNo special prep needed
What to wearLoose top or blouse
CT Imaging

CT Coronary Angiography & Calcium Scoring

A CT scan that creates detailed 3D images of your coronary arteries, showing whether there is narrowing or calcium deposits that may increase your risk of heart disease. It is non-invasive — no catheters or incisions — and significantly faster than a traditional angiogram.

Duration30–60 minutes
Comfortable?Mild — cannula in arm
PreparationFasting may be required — we'll advise
NoteContains contrast dye (iodine)
Monitoring

Heart rhythm monitoring

These tests record your heart's electrical activity over a period of time — from a single day to several weeks.

Monitoring

24-Hour ECG (Holter Monitor)

Small electrodes are attached to your chest and connected to a recorder you carry for 24 hours (sometimes longer). The recorder captures your heart's electrical activity continuously, allowing us to detect abnormal rhythms that come and go. You carry on with your normal activities.

Worn for24–48 hours
Comfortable?Yes — minor restriction
PreparationShower beforehand — no bathing while wearing
Good to knowYou keep a diary of symptoms
Monitoring

Patch Heart Recorder

A small, discreet waterproof patch worn directly on the skin — no wires, no leads. It records your heart rhythm continuously for up to several weeks, making it ideal for catching infrequent palpitations or episodes that don't show up in a 24-hour monitor. You can shower, sleep, and exercise normally while wearing it.

Worn forUp to 4 weeks
Comfortable?Yes — waterproof & discreet
PreparationNo special preparation needed
Good to knowNormal life continues throughout
Functional testing

Stress & exercise tests

These tests assess how your heart performs under physical or pharmacological stress.

Exercise

Exercise Stress Test (Treadmill ECG)

You walk on a treadmill while your heart's electrical activity is monitored continuously. The speed and incline gradually increase. This allows us to see how your heart responds to physical demand — detecting changes that may not appear at rest, such as exercise-induced arrhythmias or reduced blood supply to the heart muscle.

Duration30–45 minutes
Comfortable?Physically demanding
PreparationWear comfortable shoes; fasting 2h before
NoteYou can stop at any time
Exercise + Imaging

Stress Echocardiogram

A combination of exercise (or pharmacological stress if exercise isn't appropriate) and echocardiography. Images of your heart are taken before and immediately after stress to compare how the heart muscle contracts under load. This is more sensitive than an ECG alone for detecting coronary artery disease.

Duration60–90 minutes
Comfortable?Moderate — brief exertion
PreparationFasting 4h before; loose clothing
NoteMedication advice given at booking
Other investigations

Further cardiac tests

Ambulatory Blood Pressure Monitoring

A blood pressure cuff worn for 24 hours that records your BP throughout the day and night. More accurate than a single clinic reading for diagnosing high blood pressure.

Duration: 24 hours worn · Preparation: None

Trans-Oesophageal Echo (TOE)

A specialised echocardiogram using a probe passed gently into the oesophagus. Provides clearer images of specific structures (particularly heart valves) than a standard echo. Sedation is used.

Duration: 1–2 hours · Fasting required

Extended Hours Appointments

We offer appointments beyond standard clinic hours — including some early mornings and evenings — to make specialist care accessible around your work and family commitments.
